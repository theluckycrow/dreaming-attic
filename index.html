The Attic

You ascend the worn wooden steps and open the door into the attic, the warmest room in the house. You're in no rush.

Dust disturbed by nothing in particular. Light from a window that cannot be located from outside — it arrives at a low angle, suggesting late afternoon, though this does not change. The floor is wide plank wood, unevenly settled over whatever century it has been settling in. It smells of cedar and something else beneath the cedar. Paper, maybe. Or the particular mineral stillness of things that have been here long enough to stop being things and start being the air around them.

There is no obvious system. There never was.
